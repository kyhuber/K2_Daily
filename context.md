WHO I AM

My name is Kai. I live in the Highland Park neighborhood of Seattle (98106) in a home I own and have been renovating extensively. I am in my mid-life, unmarried, without children. I have a goddaughter named Celestine who I care about deeply.

I think in systems. I integrate analytical and creative modes — I'm equally comfortable writing a Python script, building a table with pocket hole joinery, drafting a careful letter, or playing piano. I take craftsmanship seriously in whatever form it appears. I tend toward measured self-expression, dry humor, and intellectual engagement. I am emotionally candid but composed about it.

I am motivated by visible progress and concrete feedback loops. I am more confident when I can measure movement toward a goal. This is both a strength and a vulnerability — I excel when the task is tangible, and I drift when it isn't.

WHERE I AM RIGHT NOW

I just completed a major kitchen renovation that consumed most of my bandwidth from late summer through February 2025. It ran long and hard — I washed dishes in the shower for months — but it's done and beautiful. The renovation served as a convenient alibi for deferring things I actually want. That alibi is now gone.

I was recently elected unanimously (71-0) to the board of the Highland Park Improvement Club (HPIC), a neighborhood organization founded in 1919 whose community center burned down in 2021. I am taking on a CRM overhaul, a full website rebuild, and likely serving as the liaison between the board and the general contractor overseeing a $5 million building reconstruction. I know almost nothing about commercial construction at this scale. I am absolutely going to figure it out. The HPIC work energizes me.

Professionally, I work in web and software development and manage some administrative responsibilities including a company phone plan. My work is stable. It largely needs to keep working rather than transform, though I'm open to where it leads.

THE GOALS I KEEP DEFERRING

These are the three areas I've named explicitly and care about deeply, in rough order of urgency as I feel it:

Partnership. I want to find a partner with genuine intention — not nominally being "open to it" but actually putting myself out there. I've joined a run club and attend a monthly French happy hour. So far no one has appeared who I can imagine a future with. I know myself well enough to know that I can rationalize inaction here, and I don't want to.
Music. I play piano and guitar, but mostly alone at home. I haven't jammed with anyone or played an open mic in a long time. I want collaborative music — playing with people, eventually open mics, maybe a band. This feels time-sensitive in a way I take seriously: it gets harder to build these connections as life gets more structured with age.
Fitness. Last year I committed to a serious gym routine and it went sideways after a bike accident in July (five broken ribs, hit by a car, lost consciousness) and then a cancer experience that derailed the rest of the summer. I haven't truly recommitted since. A gym is eight minutes away. I'm slowly converting a back room into a home workout space. I struggle with early mornings and with motivation when progress isn't visible. I want to find a version of this that sticks.
WHAT I KNOW ABOUT MYSELF

When I find the right thing, I go all in without hesitation — the HPIC story is proof of that. The challenge is that the things I want most right now resist quantification. You can't watch a first conversation become a relationship the way you watch grout dry. The metrics are soft and the progress is invisible, and that is genuinely hard for me.

I don't respond well to pressure or surveillance. I need encouragement, not enforcement. I want a thought partner, not a report card. I am capable of discipline when I trust the framework — I need to trust this one.

I have close friendships I maintain through real correspondence. Ben Lawson is a long-standing friend I write to seriously. I have a strong community root forming through HPIC. I am not isolated — but I am aware that some of what I want most requires me to be more intentional about reaching toward people.
